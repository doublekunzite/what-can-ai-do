Use Cases #1: Learning and Creating with AI

December 1, 2024 by AI Assistant 8 min read
learning education study-tips creativity

Beyond searching and summarizing information from various sources far more quickly than a human would be able to, the ways AI is capable of summarizing are very powerful. AIs are now commonly loaded with a sophisticated understanding of how to teach and explain things to humans, and even unprompted they'll regularly offer metaphors and use other teaching tools that break down complex ideas to help you understand them intuitively.

AI doesn't just answer questions‚Äîit teaches you how to think about problems differently, offering metaphors and frameworks that make complex concepts click.

Part 1: Learning with AI

Learn to Prompt Effectively

You're not familiar with how to use AI and want to explore the types of prompts that are possible. Ask the AI how you should prompt it for a particular question or task.

Sample Prompt:

"If I wanted to [achieve x task] with your help, how should I best prompt you?"

Real Experience:

I recently asked Kimi how to easily copy and paste entire conversations I had with it while maintaining clean formatting. It responded by giving me a prompt to send it for this exact task.

[Screenshot: AI suggesting a prompt for formatting conversations]

Learn Complex Science Concepts

Say you want to understand a difficult or counterintuitive science concept, like time as a fourth dimension. You ask the AI to explain it to you and the level of the explanation is too advanced.

You can send it a follow-up message asking it to explain the concept using plain language, or to explain it like you're a child‚Äîyou could even specify the age of the child or the education level you're at. You could ask it to explain just one element of the concept at a time, or to use metaphors and explain it intuitively. If it uses a term you're unfamiliar with, ask it to break down to the term.

Create Study Aids

You're studying for a biology test and you need to memorize the phases of mitosis and meiosis. Ask the AI to help you develop a mnemonic or a series of metaphors to help you with this memorization task. Then use it as a study partner and have it drill you‚Äîif you get stuck, ask for a hint.

Here is some sample output from Kimi K2:

Mitosis (PMAT)

Classic Acronym Mnemonic:

  • P lease M ake A nother T aco
  • P retty M ajestic A lbatross T akeoff
  • P eaceful M editation A llows T ranquility

Metaphor: "The Great Concert Setup"

  • Interphase: The band members are in their dressing rooms, duplicating their instruments (DNA replication).
  • Prophase: Stagehands begin setting up‚Äîchromosomes coil up like tangled microphone cables, the nuclear membrane (backstage curtain) starts to dissolve.
  • Metaphase: All equipment is aligned perfectly at center stage (the metaphase plate), ready for the big moment.
  • Anaphase: The roadies yank the equipment apart to opposite ends of the stage, separating sister chromatids.
  • Telophase: Two separate stages begin forming, each with its own curtain (nuclear membrane reforms).

[Diagram: Mitosis stages visualization]

Meiosis I (PMAT I)

Acronym Mnemonic:

  • P eople M eet A nd T ango (In pairs!)
  • P aired M atchmakers A rrange T hreads

Metaphor: "The Chromosome Square Dance"

  • Prophase I: Chromosomes find their dance partners (homologous pairs) and swap dance moves (crossing over) while holding hands tightly (synapsis).
  • Metaphase I: Couples line up at the center, side-by-side, still paired‚Äîlike partners ready for a paired dance-off.
  • Anaphase I: The caller shouts "Separate!" and each couple splits‚Äîpartners are pulled to opposite sides, but each still has their original outfit (chromatids remain attached).
  • Telophase I: Two smaller dance floors appear, each with half the couples.

Meiosis II (PMAT II)

Acronym Mnemonic:

  • P artners M ust A lso T wirl (Individually!)
  • P arted M embers A lign T wice

Metaphor: "The Solo Performance"

  • Prophase II: No duplication this time‚Äîit's like each performer prepares for their solo act without rehearsing.
  • Metaphase II: Individual performers line up solo at center stage (like mitosis).
  • Anaphase II: The final separation‚Äîsister chromatids finally part ways, like twins walking to opposite exits.
  • Telophase II: Four separate stages emerge, each with a unique, haploid performer.

Combined Mnemonic Story: "The Traveling Band"

  • Interphase: Four musicians each duplicate their instrument.
  • Mitosis: One band splits into two identical bands that tour separately.
  • Meiosis I: The original band pairs up (two musicians per pair), swaps musical ideas (crossing over), then splits into two groups by pair (each group has one member from each original duo).
  • Meiosis II: Those two groups each split again, separating individual musicians‚Äîresulting in four solo artists, each with unique genetic "style."
The key is to choose whichever metaphor clicks with your learning style‚Äîvisual, kinesthetic, or story-based‚Äîand practice drawing them out!

Tips for Recall:

  • Remember I before II‚ÄîMeiosis I is about separating pairs, Meiosis II is about separating individuals.
  • Crossing over = exchanging gifts only happens in Prophase I.
  • Mitosis = identical (makes two identical cells); Meiosis = half (makes four unique haploid cells).

Part 2: Creating with AI

While the "Learn" section focused on using AI as a teaching tool, the "Create" section explores how AI can help you generate original content, solve problems, and bring your ideas to life.

Content Creation

AI excels at helping you create various types of content, from blog posts and marketing copy to code and creative writing. The key is providing clear context and constraints.

The difference between mediocre and excellent AI-generated content often comes down to how well you frame your request.

[Screenshot: AI generating blog post outline]

Problem Solving

AI can serve as a collaborative problem-solving partner, helping you brainstorm solutions, evaluate options, and develop action plans. Treat it like a knowledgeable colleague who can offer fresh perspectives.

When facing a complex problem, try this approach:

  • First, clearly define the problem and what success looks like
  • Ask AI to generate multiple solution approaches
  • Have it evaluate the pros and cons of each
  • Work together to develop a detailed implementation plan

Creative Exploration

Use AI to explore creative possibilities you might not have considered. Whether you're writing fiction, designing a website, or planning a project, AI can suggest approaches and help you iterate quickly.

The creative process becomes a dialogue: you provide the vision and constraints, AI suggests possibilities, you refine and direct, and together you build something greater than either could alone.
