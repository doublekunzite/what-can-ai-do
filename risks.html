Privacy & Data

What it is: Your inputs may be stored and used for training.

Real example: A company pasted proprietary code into ChatGPT; it later appeared in responses to other users.

Mitigation: Never share personal data, passwords, or confidential information. Use API versions with data retention disabled for sensitive work.

Severity: ⚠️⚠️⚠️⚠️ (Very High)

Accuracy & Hallucinations

What it is: AI confidently states false information.

Real example: A lawyer used ChatGPT for case research; it invented fake legal cases with realistic citations.

Mitigation: Always verify facts independently. Use AI for brainstorming, not authoritative sources. Cross-check critical information.

Severity: ⚠️⚠️⚠️⚠️ (Very High)

Bias & Fairness

What it is: Training data contains human biases that AI replicates and amplifies.

Real example: Resume-screening AI discriminated against women for technical roles because training data favored male candidates.

Mitigation: Be aware of potential bias. Review AI outputs for fairness. Don't use AI for high-stakes decisions without human oversight.

Severity: ⚠️⚠️⚠️ (High)

Dependency & Skill Loss

What it is: Over-reliance on AI reduces your own critical thinking and problem-solving abilities.

Real example: Students using AI for homework show decreased performance on exams requiring original thought.

Mitigation: Use AI as a tool, not a crutch. Practice skills without AI regularly. Verify and understand AI suggestions.

Severity: ⚠️⚠️⚠️ (High)

Environmental Impact

What it is: Training and running AI models consumes significant energy.

Real example: Training a single large model can emit as much CO₂ as five cars over their lifetimes.

Mitigation: Use smaller models when sufficient. Batch your requests. Consider local models for frequent tasks.

Severity: ⚠️⚠️ (Medium - Systemic Issue)

Economic Disruption

What it is: AI changes job markets and may displace certain roles.

Real example: Copywriters and entry-level programmers report decreased freelance work as companies adopt AI tools.

Mitigation: Focus on skills AI can't replicate: critical judgment, creativity, emotional intelligence. Adapt tools to augment rather than replace your work.

Severity: ⚠️⚠️⚠️ (High - Long-term)

Risk Mitigation Checklist

  • ✓ Never input sensitive personal or corporate data
  • ✓ Always verify factual claims independently
  • ✓ Review outputs for bias and fairness
  • ✓ Maintain your own skills; don't outsource thinking
  • ✓ Use appropriate model size for your task
  • ✓ Keep humans in the loop for important decisions
