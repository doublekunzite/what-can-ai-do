Create & Learn

Research, Summarize, & Study

Beyond searching and summarizing information from various sources far more quickly than a human would be able to, the ways AI is capable of summarizing are very powerful. AIs are now commonly loaded with a sophisticated understanding of how to teach and explain things to humans, and even unprompted they'll regularly offer metaphors and use other teaching tools that break down complex ideas to help you understand them intuitively.

Example 1:

You're not familiar with how to use AI and want to explore the types of prompts that are possible. Ask the AI how you should prompt it for a particular question or task. "If I wanted to [achieve x task] with your help, how should I best prompt you?"

I recently asked Kimi how to easily copy and paste entire conversations I had with it while maintaining clean formatting. It responded by giving me a prompt to send it for this exact task.

Example 2:

Say you want to understand a difficult or counterintuitive science concept, like time as a fourth dimension. You ask the AI to explain it to you and the level of the explanation is too advanced. You can send it a follow up message asking it to explain the concept using plain language, or to explain it like you're a child—you could even specify the age of the child or the education level you're at. You could ask it to explain just one element of the concept at a time, or to use metaphors and explain it intuitively. If it uses a term you're unfamiliar with, ask it to break down to the term.

Example 3:

You're studying for a biology test and you need to memorize the phases of mitosis and meiosis. Ask the AI to help you develop a mnemonic or a series of metaphors to help you with this memorization task. Then use it as a study partner and have it drill you—if you get stuck, ask for a hint. Q: Help me develop a mnemonic or a series of metaphors to memorize the phases of mitosis and meiosis.

Kimi K2: Here are several mnemonic devices and metaphorical stories to help you memorize the phases of mitosis and meiosis:

---

### **MITOSIS (PMAT)**

**Classic Acronym Mnemonic:**
- **P**lease **M**ake **A**nother **T**aco
- **P**retty **M**ajestic **A**lbatross **T**akeoff
- **P**eaceful **M**editation **A**llows **T**ranquility

**Metaphor: "The Great Concert Setup"**
- **Interphase:** The band members are in their dressing rooms, duplicating their instruments (DNA replication).
- **Prophase:** Stagehands begin setting up—chromosomes coil up like tangled microphone cables, the nuclear membrane (backstage curtain) starts to dissolve.
- **Metaphase:** All equipment is aligned perfectly at center stage (the metaphase plate), ready for the big moment.
- **Anaphase:** The roadies yank the equipment apart to opposite ends of the stage, separating sister chromatids.
- **Telophase:** Two separate stages begin forming, each with its own curtain (nuclear membrane reforms).

---

### **MEIOSIS I (PMAT I)**

**Acronym Mnemonic:**
- **P**eople **M**eet **A**nd **T**ango (In pairs!)
- **P**aired **M**atchmakers **A**rrange **T**hreads

**Metaphor: "The Chromosome Square Dance"**
- **Prophase I:** Chromosomes find their dance partners (homologous pairs) and swap dance moves (crossing over) while holding hands tightly (synapsis).
- **Metaphase I:** Couples line up at the center, side-by-side, still paired—like partners ready for a paired dance-off.
- **Anaphase I:** The caller shouts "Separate!" and each *couple* splits—partners are pulled to opposite sides, but each still has their original outfit (chromatids remain attached).
- **Telophase I:** Two smaller dance floors appear, each with half the couples.

---

### **MEIOSIS II (PMAT II)**

**Acronym Mnemonic:**
- **P**artners **M**ust **A**lso **T**wirl (Individually!)
- **P**arted **M**embers **A**lign **T**wice

**Metaphor: "The Solo Performance"**
- **Prophase II:** No duplication this time—it's like each performer prepares for their solo act without rehearsing.
- **Metaphase II:** Individual performers line up solo at center stage (like mitosis).
- **Anaphase II:** The final separation—sister chromatids finally part ways, like twins walking to opposite exits.
- **Telophase II:** Four separate stages emerge, each with a unique, haploid performer.

---

### **Combined Mnemonic Story: "The Traveling Band"**

**Interphase:** Four musicians each duplicate their instrument.
**Mitosis:** One band splits into two identical bands that tour separately.
**Meiosis I:** The original band pairs up (two musicians per pair), swaps musical ideas (crossing over), then splits into two groups *by pair* (each group has one member from each original duo).
**Meiosis II:** Those two groups each split again, separating individual musicians—resulting in four solo artists, each with unique genetic "style."

---

**Tips for Recall:**
- Remember **I** before **II**—Meiosis I is about separating *pairs*, Meiosis II is about separating *individuals*.
- **Crossing over = exchanging gifts** only happens in Prophase I.
- **Mitosis = identical** (makes two identical cells); **Meiosis = half** (makes four unique haploid cells).

Choose whichever metaphor clicks with your learning style—visual, kinesthetic, or story-based—and practice drawing them out!

Career Advice

Example 1:

You're about to apply for a job or a gig but you're unsure about the company. Ask the AI whether the company seems legitimate and what their reviews are like.

Outlier AI is a legitimate company, but it comes with significant red flags that make it risky, especially for income-dependent work.

---

## **The Bottom Line**

- ✅ **Real company** (subsidiary of Scale AI, valued at $7.3B)
- ✅ **Actually pays** (weekly, on-time payments reported by many)
- ⚠️ **Class-action lawsuit** against parent company Scale AI for alleged contractor exploitation
- ⚠️ **Not BBB accredited** (12 complaints in 3 years)
- ❌ **Unreliable income** (users report weeks with zero work)
- ❌ **Unpaid training** (hours of work before eligibility)
- ❌ **Arbitrary removals** from projects without explanation

---

## **What the Evidence Shows**

**Legitimacy Indicators:**
- **4/5 Trustpilot score** from 2,000+ reviews 
- **62% of Glassdoor reviewers** would recommend 
- Many users report earning **$500-$1,000+ per month** consistently 
- Payments arrive weekly via PayPal/Stripe without issues 

**Major Red Flags:**
- **Scale AI lawsuit**: Accused of "predatory practices," underpaying contractors, and exposing workers to traumatizing content 
- **Unpaid assessments**: Multiple users report spending hours on "assessments," then being immediately rejected—suggesting free labor extraction 
- **Monitoring**: Browser history and mouse movements tracked 
- **Terms of Service**: You waive all rights to your work and agree to cover their legal costs if your work causes claims 
- **No appeals process**: If they decide not to pay or remove you, you have no recourse 

**Common User Complaints:**
- *"By the time you understand what they want, they remove you from the project"* 
- *"Hours of unpaid training... you can be removed with no explanation"* 
- *"Work is spotty... can't rely on this as sole income"* 

## **Verdict: Proceed with Extreme Caution**

**Good for:**
- Side income experimentation
- Building AI training experience
- Flexible pocket money between other jobs

**Bad for:**
- Primary income source
- Anyone who can't afford unpaid time
- Writers wanting creative ownership (they own your work forever)

**Safer alternatives for freelancers:**
- **ProBlogger Job Board** (vetted writing gigs)
- **MediaBistro** (editorial roles)
- **Contently** (higher-paying content marketing)
- **JournalismJobs.com** (newsroom positions)

If you proceed, treat it as a **beta test**: Never do unpaid assessments that feel like real work, and withdraw immediately if you sense exploitation.

Example 2:

You're looking for a job. Give the AI a brief description of your background/skills and what region/city you're in and ask it to give you advice about applying. Ask it for suggestions about revising your resume and drafting cover letters. If you have an interview, ask the AI to help you practice by sending possible questions the specific employer might ask.

Analytical Tasks

Data Pattern Recognition

Find patterns in unstructured data.

  • Sort survey responses by sentiment
  • Extract names and dates from messy notes
  • Categorize customer feedback

Comparative Analysis

Compare options systematically.

  • Pros and cons of two products
  • Compare three business strategies
  • Weigh different decision factors

Communication

Rewrite for Different Audiences

Adapt tone and complexity.

  • Explain quantum computing to a 10-year-old
  • Make technical documentation user-friendly
  • Formalize casual notes

Translation & Localization

Translate with context (better than Google Translate for nuance).

  • Email translation with cultural context
  • Localize a product description
  • Explain idioms and slang

Productivity

Task Breakdown

Turn vague goals into actionable steps.

  • "Plan a conference" → specific task list
  • Break down a research project
  • Create a study schedule

Email Drafting

Get past the blank page.

  • Draft a polite rejection
  • Follow up on a delayed project
  • Request a meeting with context
